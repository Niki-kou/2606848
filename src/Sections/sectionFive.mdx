When mapped out in a timeline, it is very easy to see how burial practises changed as time went on, shifting along with its society’s belief and situation at the moment. In the neolithic rituals and objects were simple, during the bronze/Mycenaean graves became more elaborate, ad during the Archaic/Classical they became even more elaborate. This shift in perspective come from Ancient Greece progressing both economically and socially, as is reflected by the higher quality funerary objects we see in graves. Of course, though, it is important to remember that most of the updates only applied to those who could afford them, and that those who couldn’t likely saw less changes in their burial sites.
